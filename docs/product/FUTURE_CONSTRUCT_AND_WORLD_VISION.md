# MXZTAR Future Construct and World Vision

## Document authority

Status: future product vision, not a Level One implementation contract.

This document preserves the long-horizon direction for MXZTAR Forge, modular 3D
construction, infrastructure building, and a persistent human-designed virtual world.
It must not be used to advertise unfinished functionality or reorder the Level One MVP
without a deliberate Master Build Plan revision.

The governing purpose is:

> Humans design the components. Humans assemble the systems. Humans build the world.
> MXZTAR provides the forge, infrastructure, provenance, and persistent spatial canvas.

AI assists lateral exploration, validation, optimisation, and automation. Human users
retain authority over acceptance, construction, destructive change, publication, and
the values embodied in what they build.

## Product-family horizon

### 1. MXZTAR Forge

```text
source material
→ structured evidence
→ human review
→ approved shapes
→ reusable Forge Pack
```

Level One establishes local project truth, provenance, safe AI execution, reviewable
production intelligence, and portable outputs.

### 2. MXZTAR Construct

```text
approved shapes
→ construction methods
→ editable components
→ compatible modules and variants
→ human-governed assemblies
→ efficient verified 3D assets
```

Construct supplies reversible 2D-to-3D operations, spatial editing, modular alignment,
connection rules, materials, lighting, mechatronic intent, and downstream profiles.

### 3. MXZTAR Infrastructure Builder

```text
assemblies
→ structures
→ utilities and transport
→ settlements
→ cities and ecosystems
→ vertically connected infrastructure
```

The infrastructure model may extend from deep geology and underground systems through
the surface, upper structures, atmosphere, and later orbital interfaces.

### 4. MXZTAR World

```text
human-authored assets and systems
→ persistent regions
→ shared settlements
→ moon-scale streamed world
→ generational construction and stewardship
```

MXZTAR World is a future persistent spatial platform where users design structures,
infrastructure, settlements, cities, and ecosystems. Persistence, authorship,
portability, collaboration, and fair rights must be designed as core architecture.

## Lateral design intelligence

Future Forge/Construct reasoning should propose inspectable possibilities such as:

- shapes within shapes;
- positive and negative space interpretations;
- panels, apertures, frames, ribs, channels, sockets, shells, and internal mechanisms;
- extruded, revolved, swept, lofted, folded, relieved, shelled, or morphed forms;
- mirrored, radial, stacked, repeated, tapered, and nested variants;
- module families that preserve compatible anchors and connection geometry;
- assemblies that emerge from several compatible components;
- alternative interpretations separated into observed, inferred, and intentionally
  invented possibilities.

The system may think laterally, but it must show its assumptions. A proposal is not
approved project truth until the user accepts it.

## Asset vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Shape | Reviewed 2D contour, mask, path, or profile |
| Feature | Hole, inset, ridge, channel, surface region, or attachment area |
| Component | Editable 3D object derived from one or more shapes |
| Module | Reusable component with connection and variation rules |
| Variant | Modified module that preserves declared compatibility |
| Instance | Positioned use of a module |
| Assembly | Multiple related components retained as distinct parts |
| Joined mesh | Mesh objects combined into one mesh object |
| Boolean result | Geometry created through union, difference, or intersection |
| Weld | Explicit visual, mesh, assembly, or manufacturing-intent connection |
| System | Assembly with structural, material, ecological, or mechatronic behaviour |

Visual or mesh welding must never be represented as certified physical or
manufacturing performance.

## Modular compatibility contract

A future reusable module may define:

- stable module and variant IDs;
- anchors and connection sockets;
- connection normals and permitted orientations;
- coordinate system, scale, and units;
- contact, clearance, and exclusion regions;
- symmetry and repeat intervals;
- compatible neighbours and adapter requirements;
- collision/intersection state;
- connection or weld intent;
- compatibility version;
- parent shape, construction recipe, and provenance.

Forge should be able to state when visual variants preserve connection compatibility.

## Modular Construct workspace

The future spatial editor may provide:

- X, Y, and Z positioning;
- perspective and orthographic views;
- numeric transforms and transform gizmos;
- global/local axes and editable pivots;
- grid, point, edge, face, axis, surface-normal, and socket snapping;
- hierarchy, locking, visibility, duplication, mirroring, arrays, and instances;
- collision, intersection, clearance, and compatibility warnings;
- undo/redo and non-destructive construction history;
- group, assembly, join mesh, boolean, separate, and bake as distinct operations;
- assisted alignment and adapters that remain user-approved.

One-click Make 3D means generating a reversible preview through a declared construction
method. It must not imply a finished production object.

## Design rendering and cyber-art efficiency

The rendering goal is:

> Maximum visual identity per unit of computation.

Every deliverable should ultimately declare a performance profile including:

- target hardware tier and frame-rate class;
- polygon/vertex and material counts;
- texture memory and resolution;
- transparency, lighting, shadow, animation, and mechatronic cost;
- collision complexity;
- expected viewing distance;
- LOD, culling, instancing, batching, and streaming policy;
- validation status.

Planned representations may include Design, Hero, Standard, Performance, Distant,
Horizon, Collision, and Infrastructure profiles.

Efficient output should favour reusable instancing, shared materials, texture atlases,
bounded transparency, baked detail where appropriate, LODs, impostors, culling,
simplified collision, streamed chunks, and explicit low/medium/high-rig targets.

## Materials, effects, and lighting

Future render controls may operate at object, material-group, face, panel, inset, edge,
and internal-component level.

Properties may include:

- surface, hue, colour, opacity, transmission, roughness, metallic response, emission,
  reflectivity, texture, edge treatment, and wear;
- studio, inspection, concept, environmental, directional, area, and emissive lighting;
- exposure, colour temperature, shadows, and reflection environment;
- hypermodern visual profiles such as brushed alloys, ceramics, composites, smart
  glass, translucent polymers, luminous panels, and layered mechatronic casings.

Visual profiles must be separated from verified engineering material properties.

## Mechatronic intent

A future Mechatronics command tree may describe:

- structure: frames, casings, mounts, service access;
- motion: hinges, sliders, rotary joints, limits;
- actuation: motors, linear actuators, conceptual drives;
- sensing: proximity, position, optical, and environmental sensing;
- routing: cables, conduits, pipes, and protected channels;
- simulation intent: motion, collision, clearance, and sequence previews.

Conceptual behaviour must not be marketed as engineering simulation until specialist
contracts and validation exist.

## UI direction

The long-term UI may replace the permanent sidebar only after complete top-menu and
command infrastructure exists.

Proposed top-level command families:

```text
File | Edit | View | Project | Source | Analyse | Shapes | Components | Construct
Materials | Lighting | Mechatronics | Automate | Export | Settings | Help
```

A command may be reachable from menus, toolbars, context menus, shortcuts, command
search, Insights, and workflow recommendations. Every route must invoke one shared
command with one validation, engine, undo, error, persistence, and verification path.

The visual identity should remain original MXZTAR hyper-grid minimalism: dark graphite,
restrained gold accents, precise grids, geometric structure, contextual controls,
clear axes and depth, minimal borders, and useful—not decorative—motion.

## Infrastructure layers

The future infrastructure builder may model:

1. deep geology, excavation, mines, reservoirs, and geothermal systems;
2. underground utilities, drainage, power, data, transport, and service tunnels;
3. surface terrain, ecosystems, agriculture, settlements, cities, and transport;
4. towers, aerial transit, suspended structures, and atmospheric systems;
5. flight corridors, communications, weather, and orbital interfaces.

Structures may connect to energy, water, waste, communications, logistics, ecology,
weather, mobility, population activity, and mechatronic systems. Simulation depth must
always be declared honestly.

## Moon-scale spatial architecture

A small-moon-scale world requires hierarchy and streaming rather than one scene:

```text
world
→ region
→ territory
→ district
→ site
→ structure
→ assembly
→ module
→ component
```

Future foundations may include planetary coordinates, origin rebasing, hierarchical
world partitioning, multi-resolution terrain, deterministic IDs, instancing, streamed
state, spatial permissions, conflict-safe edits, server authority, offline Forge Packs,
backups, migrations, and recoverable provenance.

## Human design, ecology, and generational value

Users may choose to build forests, farms, underground settlements, research towns,
walking cities, vertical cyber-cities, transport systems, cultural archives, machine
ecologies, atmospheric habitats, or other human-conceived places.

Ecological design may later represent planting regions, species, density, water,
growth stages, canopy, shade, seasons, and relationships with infrastructure. Visual
ecology and scientifically validated simulation must remain distinct claims.

AI may propose, compare, optimise, and test. Humans decide what exists, what is
preserved, what changes, and what future users inherit.

## Authorship, ownership, and fair rights

“Users own what they design” requires precise terms separating:

- user source-material rights;
- authorship of shapes, components, systems, and exports;
- rights in AI-assisted derivatives;
- permission to place work in a shared world;
- collaboration and contributor rights;
- platform display/simulation licences;
- parcel or spatial-control rights;
- removal, migration, and service-closure rights.

The platform must not call revocable database access “land ownership.” Formal rights,
licences, moderation, collaboration, and succession rules require legal review before
public promises.

## Persistence beyond one cloud service

A generational construct cannot rely on one live database. Users should be able to
retain portable Forge Packs, component libraries, assembly/region manifests,
authorship records, revision histories, world snapshots, open-format exports, and local
backups.

The shared world persists through maintained infrastructure. Human-created assets and
their histories should remain portable and recoverable beyond any single deployment.

## Present-day architectural consequences

This future strengthens, but does not reorder, the current build:

1. stable project, artifact, shape, component, and parent IDs;
2. explicit observed/inferred/user-approved states;
3. source-to-derivative provenance;
4. non-destructive history and supersession;
5. target units, coordinates, anchors, and compatibility versions;
6. portable readable outputs;
7. rig-aware performance metadata;
8. human authority over consequential operations.
