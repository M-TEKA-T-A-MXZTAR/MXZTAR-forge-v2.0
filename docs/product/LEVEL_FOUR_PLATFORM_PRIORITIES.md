# MXZTAR Level Four Platform Priorities

## Document authority

Status: long-horizon product priority, not an implemented feature contract.

Planning horizon: no earlier than approximately one year from 20 July 2026, and only
after Levels One through Three have passed their own verified acceptance gates. Time is
an estimate, not a release promise.

This document defines the intended Level Four MVP for the wider MXZTAR Forge platform.
It does not reorder the current Level One build, authorise cloud infrastructure spending,
or advertise browser, AR, VR, collaboration, monetisation, therapeutic, or persistent-
world functions as available today.

## Core identity

MXZTAR Forge begins with source art, but its long-term asset is larger:

> A human-first creative civilisation builder that turns personal source material into
> authored, inspectable, portable components—and eventually into persistent worlds that
> people can construct, understand, revise, and pass forward.

The platform must not become merely another image generator, automatic 3D converter, or
game-engine wrapper. Its distinctive value is the bridge between:

- personal creative authority and shared construction;
- lateral AI assistance and decisive human authorship;
- modest local hardware and optional collaborative infrastructure;
- individual source provenance and generational design history;
- static assets and living systems of relationships, ecology, infrastructure, and time.

Humans decide what becomes project truth, what enters a shared world, what changes, and
what is removed. Automation may prepare, compare, validate, notify, and recommend. It may
not bypass consequential approval gates.

## Market gaps Level Four should close

### The shallow-or-heavy divide

Many creative tools are either fast but shallow, or powerful but difficult to enter.
Level Four should preserve a progressive path: inspect a Forge Pack, place a component,
respond to one operator job, then graduate into deeper construction when the user needs
it. Advanced capability should not require every user to become a game-engine specialist.

### Weak portable authorship

Collaborative worlds commonly preserve current state better than human design history.
MXZTAR should treat source identity, contributor credit, decisions, revisions,
supersession, licences, and parent-child provenance as travelling parts of the asset.

### Cloud dependence and hardware exclusion

The platform should retain useful local and offline operation, explicit rig profiles,
bounded previews, efficient representations, and portable deltas. Optional cloud
collaboration must extend local authority rather than replace it.

### Static-prop thinking

Trees, habitats, utilities, transport, settlements, and machines are systems—not merely
decorations. Level Four should be able to represent growth, dependencies, service paths,
relationships, state changes, stewardship, and declared simulation limits.

### Missing generational history

Long-lived worlds need inspectable time: what existed, who proposed a change, who
approved it, what it replaced, what depended upon it, and which snapshot can be restored.
Generational change and design history are first-class product data.

## Level Four MVP acceptance boundary

Level Four is an operational cross-device platform milestone, not the completion of the
moon-scale vision. Its MVP is complete only when a verified user journey can:

1. open one portable Forge Pack through a supported browser or tablet experience;
2. inspect authorship, provenance, permissions, compatibility, and performance metadata;
3. view approved assets in a bounded light-3D region;
4. place or propose placement of a compatible module without bypassing authority;
5. receive and resolve an operator job through an explicit human decision;
6. exchange a versioned cloud delta without overwriting newer local or shared truth;
7. inspect the same proposal through at least one immersive review surface;
8. recover the personal asset archive independently of the shared-world service;
9. reconstruct who proposed, approved, changed, or rejected the world-state delta;
10. export the user-controlled asset and its travelling provenance in documented formats.

Level Four MVP does not promise a finished moon-scale civilisation simulation, unlimited
concurrency, scientifically validated ecology, engineering certification, legal land
ownership, guaranteed income, medical or therapeutic outcomes, or permanent service
operation.

## Platform product contracts

### Browser and tablet — core entry point

The browser/tablet contract may provide:

- Forge Pack and provenance inspection;
- 2D and bounded light-3D review;
- Shape Library and approved-module browsing;
- basic compatible placement and proposal drafting;
- operator-job inbox, evidence, decision, and audit trail;
- account, permission, archive, and sync-status visibility.

It is not the default surface for unbounded local model work or heavy geometry processing.

### Mobile and AR — capture and situated review

The mobile/AR contract may provide:

- deliberate capture into a local intake queue;
- lightweight source preflight before Forge analysis;
- scale-explicit AR preview of approved assets;
- field notes, measurements identified by method and uncertainty;
- operator jobs tied to a place, asset, or proposal.

AR appearance must not be represented as engineering fit, safe installation, ownership,
or planning approval.

### VR — immersive construction and review

The VR contract may provide:

- spatial inspection and compatible component placement;
- human-scale and environment-scale walkthroughs;
- collaborative review sessions with visible participant identity;
- proposal comparison, annotation, and approval routing;
- comfort, accessibility, performance, and session-safety controls.

VR interaction produces proposals and authorised changes through the same command and
validation contracts as every other surface.

### Desktop Forge — authoritative heavy lifting

The desktop contract remains responsible for:

- local source analysis and heavy processing;
- evidence correction, approval, rejection, and supersession;
- shape, component, assembly, and Forge Pack production;
- export orchestration and specialist-tool handoff;
- local archive, recovery, validation, and performance profiling.

Desktop authority must remain usable without compulsory participation in a shared world.

## Shared cross-platform contract

Every supported surface must respect the same durable rules:

- local authority and offline work remain meaningful;
- every asset and delta has a stable identity and schema version;
- provenance, authorship, permissions, and licences travel with the asset;
- human approval gates are explicit and cannot be silently bypassed;
- destructive and world-visible actions require validated authority;
- conflicts never become silent last-write-wins data loss;
- uncertain, stale, blocked, and recovery states remain visibly distinct;
- cloud synchronisation is optional, disclosed, encrypted, resumable, and auditable;
- no device route creates a second hidden command or weaker validation path;
- users can export their controlled assets in documented portable forms.

## Operator-job system

An operator job is a durable request for human input or approval. It turns automation
into accountable assistance rather than invisible control.

Possible jobs include:

- review a new bridge or placement proposal;
- resolve a module-compatibility conflict;
- approve a collaborator's world-state delta;
- examine an ecosystem or infrastructure warning;
- choose between alternative repairs or adapters;
- confirm licensing, publication, or shared-world permission;
- review a migration, archive restore, or destructive change.

Every operator job should declare:

- job and project/world IDs;
- triggering event and evidence;
- affected assets, dependencies, region, and users;
- requested decision and available options;
- authority required and current holder;
- risk, reversibility, deadline where legitimate, and consequence of no action;
- model or rule involvement;
- created, viewed, decided, expired, and superseded timestamps;
- final human decision and resulting durable event.

Notifications may request attention. They must not manufacture urgency or convert silence
into approval. No response leaves the job undecided and blocks every consequential,
destructive, or world-visible change. A separately governed safety rule may take only a
defined fail-closed or other non-approval action, with its evidence recorded for review.

## Portable personal archive

The archive is a core user-owned capability, not a service-closure afterthought. It should
retain source assets where rights permit, Forge Packs, shapes, components, assemblies,
authorship records, licences, operator decisions, deltas, snapshots, and readable indexes.

The user should be able to validate and restore the archive without the live world
database. Service shutdown, account closure, moderation, or regional removal must not
silently erase the user's lawful portable work.

## Authorship economy and voluntary value exchange

Level Four may support clear credit, reusable-module licensing, collaboration terms,
optional paid assets, commissions, or creator support. Any economy must be downstream of
verified authorship, rights, provenance, moderation, tax, refund, payment, dispute, and
consumer-law contracts.

The platform must not promise income or label revocable platform access as ownership.
Users need plain-language visibility into what they control, what they licence, what the
platform may display, and what remains portable if a shared placement is removed.

## Educational, reflective, and wellbeing value

World-building may support learning, planning, self-expression, cultural storytelling,
reflection, collaboration, and constructive community activity. Those are meaningful
uses. They are not medical treatment claims.

Future guided experiences should protect privacy, avoid manipulative dependency, support
accessible pacing, and clearly separate creative wellbeing from clinically validated
therapy. Cultural material requires creator authority, appropriate attribution, and
respectful governance rather than automated extraction.

## Multi-device delta model

Cross-device work should exchange small, signed or authorised, schema-valid changes rather
than repeatedly replacing whole worlds. A delta should identify:

- base snapshot and target scope;
- author, device, session, and authority;
- commands and affected stable IDs;
- parent versions and dependency expectations;
- provenance and licence effects;
- validation and compatibility results;
- conflicts, approvals, and final application event.

Offline deltas remain proposals until reconciled against authoritative current state.

## Rig-efficient world principle

The platform should maximise authored visual identity per unit of computation. Level Four
inherits Forge's hardware kindness through instancing, LODs, atlases, culling, streamed
regions, bounded transparency, simplified collision, reusable materials, and declared
device profiles.

Every immersive asset should state its intended device class and measured or estimated
cost. Better hardware may unlock richer representations; it must not silently multiply
heavy jobs or make the user's archive unusable on modest equipment.

## Gated dependency order

Level Four work may begin only after the preceding assets exist:

1. Level One: operational local Forge, project truth, reviewed intelligence, Shape
   Library, Jobs, Forge Pack, recovery, and release engineering;
2. Level Two: reversible component generation and verified Construct workspace;
3. Level Three: assemblies, infrastructure relationships, regional state, performance
   profiles, and offline snapshot/delta foundations;
4. Level Four: cross-device contracts, operator jobs, optional shared authority,
   immersive review, portable archives, and the first persistent region.

The first Level Four delivery should be one small, recoverable shared region—not a hollow
moon-scale claim. Scale expands only after authority, portability, conflict handling,
performance, moderation, backup, and restore have all produced evidence.

## Level Four proof gates

Before Level Four can be called an MVP, evidence must include:

- cross-device schema and round-trip tests;
- offline edit and conflict-reconciliation tests;
- operator-job authority and no-response tests;
- provenance and licence continuity tests;
- archive export, validation, deletion, and restore drills;
- multi-user permission and destructive-action tests;
- low-, medium-, and high-rig performance profiles;
- accessibility and immersive comfort checks;
- privacy, security, moderation, and New Zealand legal review;
- service-interruption and shared-region recovery tests.

The immediate lever remains Level One: finish the local, durable Forge asset before
building the network that will eventually carry it.
